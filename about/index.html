Where am I?

Welcome to seest, a tool for practicing your estimates on the time needed to finish an activity. It provides input for an estimate, a timer to measure the actual time taken, and the comparison between the two.

Why bother?

Because your time is a limited resource and schedules can increase efficiency. A common mistake, however, is to make a schedule based on what you want to do and not what you can do. If your body needs 10 minutes to jog 1 mile, no amount of time management is going to let you finish 1 mile in 1 minute. To figure out reasonable time slots, you will need to accurately estimate how long it takes to do things.

How do I Use this?

Form

The form contains an activity and estimate field. The activity is optional and is a reminder for what you are estimating. The estimate is your guess at how long it will take to finish the activity. It follows a special format #h#m#s where #h is the hours, #m is the minutes, #s is the seconds, and # is a non-negative whole number. You can leave out a unit (e.g. 1h30m) and you can go over 60 seconds or minutes (e.g. 90m). Once you're ready press the “Start” button to begin the timer.

Timer

The timer page lists your activity and your estimate, along with a timer of how long it has been since you started. When you're done with your task, press the “Finish” button to see the results.

Results

The results page is a summary of your session. In particular you will want to pay attention to the error and difference so you can make adjustments for future estimates.

Error The percentage your estimate is off by relative to the duration
(estimate - duration) ÷ duration × 100
Duration Amount of time the activity took
Estimate Amount of time estimated for the activity
Difference Amount of time to adjust the estimate by
duration − estimate
Started When the timer started
Ended When the timer ended

In case you are repeating activities, the “New” button will send you back to the form with the same values already filled in.

Notes

Except for the form page, your session information is saved as part of the URL so you can send it or sync it with your other devices. You can also bookmark pages if you want to revisit them later or you want to close the browser. If you are confident you will not clear your history, you can just simply close it and open it back from history instead.

If you wish to have a link to the form with its fields automatically filled in, first fill in the form, start the timer, immediately end the timer, and use the “New” button from the results page. The URL of the current form page will have the values you entered from the beginning.

Any tips?

Plan your activity

Be specific about what you are doing and what your goals are. The process of defining the activity in greater detail encourages further visualization of the situation, which will help you identify any aspects that may have been overlooked. In addition, it will be painfully obvious when you have been distracted and start working off tangent.

Know what you want

Have concrete conditions for being done. For some activities this may be trivial but for others, where there is no finished state, more work can always go in because it can always be made better. Without knowing beforehand what you want, it will be hard to predict when you will stop.

Don't get distracted

The timer does not have a pause button for a reason. If you get distracted or interrupted, nothing changes the fact that you are not done with what you planned to do. However, it is understandable if something urgent comes up and stops you from being able to continue. It is up to you to decide if such interruptions are normal and whether they should be considered in future planning.
